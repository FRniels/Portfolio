# Reflection

## Korthagen Reflection Model

### Action

This year, PXL organised an on-campus job event intended to connect students with potential employers. As an Electronics-ICT student, I attended with the hope of making meaningful contact with companies active in the electronics or embedded systems sector, and potentially exploring future career opportunities.

I walked the event floor and visited a number of stands. The one conversation that stood out was with the UHasselt stand, where I had a genuine discussion about the possibility of pursuing a master's degree at a later stage in life. As someone who is 34 and still interested in continuing into a master's programme, that specific conversation felt relevant and worthwhile.

### Looking Back

Much like a previous industry visit, the gap between expectation and reality was the defining experience of this event. The electronics sector was notably underrepresented — if there were two electronics-focused companies present, that would have been a generous count. The majority of exhibitors operated in fields outside my area of study and career interest, which made it difficult to have any meaningful technical or professional conversations.

The UHasselt stand was the clear exception. The conversation there was directly relevant to my personal situation and longer-term academic ambitions, making it the most valuable interaction of the day by a significant margin. Outside of that, the event did not offer much traction for someone specifically looking to land an electronics-focused role.

### Awareness of Essential Aspects

The event highlighted that a general job fair — even one organised within a technical university college — does not necessarily reflect the needs of every programme equally. For students in business, IT services, or more commercially oriented fields, an event like this likely offers plenty of relevant contacts. For someone with a specific interest in electronics engineering, the landscape looked quite different.

It also reinforced something more personal: that my goal of pursuing a master's degree is a realistic and worthwhile direction to explore, and that institutions like UHasselt are open to non-traditional student profiles. That alone gave the visit some purpose.

### Creating Alternatives

A more targeted event — one specifically oriented toward electronics, embedded systems, and hardware engineering companies — would be significantly more valuable for students in this field. Even a smaller, curated event with five or ten relevant exhibitors would outperform a large general fair with no representation of the sector.

In the meantime, industry-specific career events, engineering trade fairs, or direct outreach to companies in the embedded and FPGA space would likely be more effective channels for making meaningful professional connections.

### Trial

Going forward, I will be more selective about which job or networking events are worth attending, and will prioritise events that are clearly aligned with the electronics and embedded systems industry. For broader fairs, I will set specific goals in advance — such as the UHasselt conversation here — so the visit has at least one defined purpose rather than an open-ended search for relevance.

## PXL X-Factor

* **(Em)passion:** Despite the limited number of relevant exhibitors, I stayed engaged and made sure to have at least one meaningful conversation — the UHasselt stand — that connected directly to my personal academic goals.
* **Entrepreneurial:** I identified the most relevant opportunity in the room and pursued it directly, rather than walking away from the event empty-handed.
* **Innovative:** The visit prompted me to think more critically about which professional events and channels are actually suited to the electronics sector, and what a better-targeted alternative might look like.
* **(Inter)nationality:** The event offered exposure to a variety of organisations and industries, broadening my awareness of the professional landscape beyond my immediate field of focus.
* **Multi-perspective:** Attending a general job fair as an electronics student gave me a clearer picture of how the field sits within the broader job market, and reinforced the value of seeking out more specialised opportunities.

## Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

### SDG 4 – Quality Education

The most personally meaningful outcome of this event was the conversation with UHasselt about master's study as a mature student. This connects directly to SDG 4, which promotes inclusive and equitable quality education and encourages lifelong learning. Exploring pathways to continue academic development at 34 is precisely the kind of non-traditional educational trajectory that SDG 4 supports.

### SDG 8 – Decent Work and Economic Growth

Job events like this one contribute to SDG 8 by facilitating connections between students and the labour market. While the electronics sector was underrepresented at this particular edition, the event's intent — helping students find meaningful employment — aligns with the goal of promoting full and productive employment and decent work for all.

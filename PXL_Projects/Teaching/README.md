# Tutoring a First-Year Electronics-ICT Student

## Korthagen Reflection Model

### Action

During my second year of the Electronics-ICT bachelor, I tutored a first-year student in Analog Electronics. We had approximately six lessons of 2 to 3 hours each, both at Corda Campus and at the student's home. The topics covered included basic mathematics, electrical quantities such as voltage, current and power, Ohm's law, series and parallel resistor networks, and Thévenin equivalents.

### Looking Back

Initially, I was motivated to help another student succeed in the program. I enjoyed explaining technical concepts and adapting my explanations to the student's level. However, I noticed that the student struggled with very basic mathematical concepts, such as formula manipulation and metric prefixes (kilo, milli, micro, etc.). Maintaining focus was also difficult, as distractions such as phone use regularly interrupted the lessons.

### Awareness of Essential Aspects

Through this experience, I realized how important a strong foundation in mathematics is for understanding electronics. I also learned that successful tutoring requires commitment and motivation from both the tutor and the student. While I could explain concepts in different ways and remain patient, learning progress remained limited when the student was not fully engaged.

### Creating Alternatives

If I were to tutor a student again, I would establish clearer expectations from the beginning regarding preparation, participation and focus during the sessions. I would also assess the student's prior knowledge more thoroughly so that learning objectives can be adapted to their starting level.

### Trial

In future tutoring situations, I will continue to use patience and clear explanations, but I will also communicate expectations more explicitly. This experience helped me develop my coaching and communication skills and taught me the importance of shared responsibility in the learning process.

## PXL X-Factor

* **(Em)passion:** I showed commitment by voluntarily helping another student and investing significant time in tutoring.
* **Entrepreneurial:** I took initiative to organize lessons and adapt explanations to the student's needs.
* **Innovative:** I searched for different ways to explain concepts when the student had difficulties understanding them.
* **(Inter)nationality:** Although this activity did not have an international aspect, it strengthened my ability to communicate and collaborate with people from different backgrounds.
* **Multi-perspective:** I learned to view learning difficulties from the perspective of the student and gained insight into the challenges beginning students may face.

## Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

### SDG 4 – Quality Education

This activity directly contributed to SDG 4 by supporting equal access to education and helping a fellow student develop knowledge and skills in electronics. Although the tutoring did not ultimately lead to the student continuing the program, the experience highlighted the importance of educational support and guidance in higher education.
